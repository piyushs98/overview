The Origin Story

I. The Foundation: Curiosity to Reverse Engineering

My journey into engineering began not in a classroom, but with the disassembly of household electronics. Growing up, I was driven by a need to understand the 'black box'—how invisible signals translated into sound, light, and motion. This early curiosity matured into a disciplined pursuit of electronics engineering during my Bachelor of Technology, where I transitioned from merely observing systems to building them. I learned early that successful engineering isn't just about functionality; it is about reliability, scalability, and the seamless integration of hardware and software.

II. The Crucible: System Validation & Hardware Integration

Moving to the United States to pursue my Master’s degree at Wichita State University marked a pivot to high-performance hardware engineering. At the National Institute for Aviation Research (NIAR), my focus shifted from theoretical design to critical system validation. I was responsible for benchmarking complex electro-mechanical systems against rigorous analytical models, ensuring that electrical designs and sensor integrations held up under real-world stress. This experience instilled in me a "zero-defect" mindset—validating that every circuit, signal, and interface performs flawlessly before it ever leaves the bench.

III. The Impact: Innovation & Optimization

Currently, at Sensor Electronic Technology, Inc. (SETi), I operate at the intersection of R&D and mass production. My role has evolved from component-level design to leading system-wide optimizations. By architecting automated test frameworks using Python and LabVIEW, I reduced manual testing time by over 50%, directly impacting throughput. My focus on root-cause analysis and process control has not only improved yield but also generated significant intellectual property, resulting in over 13 patent applications. I don't just maintain systems; I engineer them to be smarter, faster, and more cost-effective.

IV. The Philosophy

Engineering is the art of making the complex feel effortless. Whether I am troubleshooting a mixed-signal PCB or defining the requirements for a new product, my approach is consistent: innovation requires both creative vision and rigorous execution. I am now focused on applying my expertise in product development, automated testing, and reliability engineering to build next-generation systems that push the boundaries of performance and efficiency for industry leaders.

SETi (Internal R&D) High-Density UVA Module Characterization
The Challenge

Validating a hyper-dense 1"x1" module containing 160 LEDs for industrial water disinfection. The extreme packing density resulted in junction temperatures exceeding 150°C–200°C, requiring rigorous validation of optical stability for submerged liquid-cooled applications.

The Engineering Solution

Engineered a fully automated test bench using LabVIEW to control precision power supplies and multimeters. Executed long-duration stress tests to map Power Retention vs. Temperature vs. Time profiles, simulating the cold thermal environment of a water disinfection facility.

Impact

Generated critical thermal-optical performance reports that confirmed the module's viability for submerged high-intensity disinfection, directly informing the product roadmap.

SETi (Customer Quality) Tesla Tail Light Thermal & Optical Validation
The Challenge

High-density LED tail light assemblies were experiencing "thermal dimming"—where excessive board temperatures reduced luminance output—risking non-compliance with daylight visibility standards.

The Engineering Solution

Executed a rigorous inspection and validation protocol. Utilized spectroradiometers and luminance meters to capture optical performance while simultaneously monitoring thermal test points on the PCB. Performed a correlation study by cross-referencing physical thermal data against SolidWorks thermal simulations.

Impact

Validated the accuracy of thermal predictive models and confirmed the module's optical performance boundaries, ensuring the design met strict automotive safety criteria for future production.

SolidWorks Simulation Spectroradiometry Thermal Validation QA/QC
